Privacy Policy

Blue Rock Procurement · Effective 10 July 2026

Blue Rock Procurement is a desktop application built by Mindsheep for a single customer, Blue Rock for General Trading Ltd. It runs entirely on the customer's own computers. Mindsheep operates no servers for this application and does not receive, store, or have access to any data the application processes.

What data the application handles

On the customer's computers, the application processes: procurement documents (purchase and service requests) retrieved from the customer's own email mailbox; the data extracted from those documents (item descriptions, quantities, codes, department names, requester names); and accounting records (vendors, accounts, bills) read from and written to the customer's QuickBooks Online company.

Where data is stored

All documents, extracted data, application settings, and QuickBooks access tokens are stored locally on the computer where the application is installed. Optionally, the customer may enable archiving of documents to the customer's own cloud storage account; that feature is off by default.

Third-party services

The application communicates, over encrypted (HTTPS/TLS) connections, only with services the customer has authorized using the customer's own accounts:

Intuit QuickBooks Online — to create bills, attach source documents, and read the company's chart of accounts and vendor list. Access is granted via Intuit's OAuth 2.0 flow by an administrator of the QuickBooks company and may be revoked at any time (see disconnecting the app).
The customer's email provider — to read incoming procurement documents from a dedicated mailbox.
Anthropic API — images of documents that cannot be read locally are submitted for text extraction, using the customer's own Anthropic account and API key.

What we do not do

The application does not sell, share, or transmit customer data to Mindsheep or to any party other than the services listed above. It contains no analytics, telemetry, or advertising components.

Data retention and deletion

Because all data is stored on the customer's own equipment and accounts, retention and deletion are entirely under the customer's control. Uninstalling the application and deleting its data folder removes all locally stored data; disconnecting the QuickBooks connection invalidates its access tokens.
